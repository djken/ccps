1 - My Doctor

1 - My Doctor

I have a doctor's appointment on Wednesday. It is just a routine checkup. The last time I had to see the doctor was in August. It was during summer vacation. I fell off my bike and broke my arm. It was very painful, but my doctor was very nice. His name is Dr. Taylor. He is funny and very smart.

He graduated from Harvard Medical School. He speaks three different languages: English, Spanish, and German. His parents are from England and Germany, but he was born in Spain. I know a lot about him because he is not only my doctor, but also my uncle. I call him Uncle Simon. Although he is a busy doctor, he visits our family often. He comes over every Christmas.

Last Christmas he took my brother and me to watch Batman. This winter I hope he takes us to the movie theater again. He also reads to us. My favorite book is Mark Twain's Adventures of Huckleberry Finn. He is the coolest uncle and doctor. I want to be like him when I grow up.

2 - A Sunny Day at the Park

2 - A Sunny Day at the Park

My family and I are going on a picnic in the park. The sun is shining, so we decided it was a good day to be outside. My mother is making sandwiches in the kitchen. She is also slicing some apples for snacks. My father has been loading a few things in the car. We are going to bring some balls and a Frisbee.

My dad is going to teach me how to play soccer. He taught my older brother too. My older brother does not want to join us today. He's a teenager now so he thinks he is too old to play at the park. I hope he changes his mind. It is going to be so much fun. My mother has been looking for our picnic basket. She did not find the basket, but she did find our picnic blanket.

My dad found the basket in the garage. After my mom packed all of the food, we said good-bye to my brother. As we were backing out of the driveway, my brother started running towards us. He said he wanted to go after all. He is going to bring a book to read. He wants to read under the trees and take a nap under the warm sun. We are going to make great memories together. I hope my dad packed the camera.

3 - Breaking a Bad Habit

3 - Breaking a Bad Habit

I have a habit of biting my fingernails. My parents would scold me every time they noticed I chewed my nails.

I should stop biting them, but it's a hard habit to break. Sometimes, I don't realize I am doing it. I usually do it when I'm feeling anxious about something. My parents say it is a bad habit because nails carry germs that make people ill. My mother tried putting nail polish on my nails.

She was hoping that it would taste bitter, but it did not work. The taste did not bother me. My dad told me to put gloves on, but that only made me hot. What shall I do?

I will keep trying to stop. I think it would be easier if my parents did not pressure me about it. My sister used to bite her nails. She does not do it anymore. Maybe, I will break the habit soon.

4 - A Car Accident
4 - A Car Accident

My grandmother was in a car accident. She was brought to the hospital in an ambulance. The nurse called my father. I could sense something was wrong.

My father looked shocked. We rushed to the hospital. The doctor said she may have broken ribs. They were going to take some x-rays soon. They discovered she had several broken ribs, but no other injuries. She is a very lucky woman.

She was wearing her seatbelt. The doctor said she may be in the hospital for a couple of weeks. She might be able to leave earlier. It depends on her recovery. She can move, but she is in a lot of pain. Her injuries could get worse if she is not careful.

She must wear a brace until her ribs heal. A nurse told us that a police officer was waiting in the waiting area. He said somebody witnessed the accident. The witness said my grandmother caused the crash. She did not hit a car.

She hit a pole. My parents don't think she can drive anymore. It is too dangerous. She might get into another accident. After she leaves the hospital, she may stay with my family while she gets physical therapy. We must take good care of her. She is very important to us.

6 - A Special Day with Mom

We went to many stores. I bought several new outfits. One store had very expensive items. I didn't buy those. That department store will have an annual sale soon. My mom said to wait until that time. I think that is a good idea.

7 - A Lost Dog
7 - A Lost Dog

We lost our dog at the park yesterday. He was on a leash. The leash was old and it ripped apart. He got loose. He was running around in the park. All of a sudden he started running towards the street. He saw a dog on the other side of the street.

We ran towards him, but he kept running in the opposite direction. We lost sight of him. He disappeared so quickly. We called his name over and over again, but we couldn't find him. My dad went home and printed some signs.

We put a generous reward on the sign. We put them on trees and light posts. We even passed them out to strangers on the street. It was getting dark. I was so worried. My mom and I stayed at our house in case he came home. I missed him so much.

I couldn't sleep all night. He always slept on my bed. In the morning, someone called and said they saw him at the park again. The man on the phone said he didn't want the reward. He just wanted us to find our dog again.

He was such a nice man. We were so thankful. My parents and I rushed to the park. Our dog was waiting at the playground. I ran towards him. This time, he ran in our direction. I was so happy! I love my dog.

He is the best dog in the world. My dad bought a stronger leash at the pet store this morning. We put the leash on him, but I don't think he will run away anymore. I think he is happy to be home and on my bed.

I think it is set for the spring of next year. I may want to audition for a part. I'm a little shy, but this will help me boost my confidence.

We received a flyer at school regarding auditions a couple of weeks ago. I should have paid more attention to it. I think I threw it away. I know rehearsals are five days a week, Monday to Friday. I'm going to talk to the choir teacher tomorrow morning.

9 - A Spelling Bee
9 - A Spelling Bee

My sister won the school spelling bee. She entered the competition with ten of her classmates. She said she was very nervous on stage. I could tell by her hands. They were shaking.

She said she guessed the winning word and won by chance. I don't think she won by chance at all. She worked hard and won with true effort. I am so proud of her. The words were very difficult. They were given by the school principal.

The principal repeated the words several times and used them in sentences. That made the contest very long. It lasted over two hours. Some people were getting tired.

Other people moved by the door and windows to get some fresh air. After she spelled the winning word, the audience stood with applause. It was a great moment for her.

There is going to be a ceremony tomorrow night. The principal will present her with a big trophy with her name on it. It is going to be a special time for my family.

10 - Cheating on a Test
10 - Cheating on a Test

My classmate, Sharon, was caught cheating. She said she didn't have time for studying last night. She thought she was going to fail the test. Our teacher scolded her and sent her to the principal's office. He was not affected by her crying.

This was not the first time she was caught cheating. She has an excuse every time. Last time, her excuse was forgetting about the test. I think the teacher is getting tired of her lying.

She is good at making up excuses. This time our teacher involved the principal. They phoned her parents. Her parents hurried to school. We could see them through the window during recess.

They didn't look happy. Sharon is not a bad student. She is actually really creative. She has a great talent for drawing. She shows us a different picture each day.

Our teacher always compliments her artwork, but he also wants her to concentrate on learning. The teacher and the principal are going to give her one more chance. If she cheats again, she will get suspended from school.

She may even have to repeat the year. They are going to continue with monitoring her situation. I hope she stops cheating. She has good potential to be a great student.

12 - Updating our Home

12 - Updating our Home

New roofs are quite expensive. She wants to hire a contractor to do the work. She wants the work to be completed quickly.

My dad wants to save money on labor by doing the work alone. My dad has a lot of experience building and fixing things. He always fixes everything that needs repairs. His friends often ask him to help build or fix their broken items. My mom is more concerned about time. She wants it done fast.

13 - Waking up Late

13 - Waking up Late

What time is it? I jumped out of bed and ran to my closet. Where is my favorite shirt? I can't find anything in my bedroom.

Why is it so messy? There are clothes all over the floor. I need to clean my room after school. I don't have time to eat breakfast, so my mother packs a small snack. Where did I put my backpack? My mother hands it to me. I look out the window and see the bus. It just passed our house.

I missed the bus again. When did it come? I think it was early today. My mom is not happy with me. Why do I keep missing the bus? What is my problem? My mother has to drive me to school. I hope she won't be late for work. She has an important meeting this morning. I appreciate everything she does for me. Tomorrow, I won't be late.
